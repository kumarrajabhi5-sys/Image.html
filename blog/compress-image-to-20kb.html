Why Do Forms Ask for Images Under 20KB?

Government portals, exam boards, and job portals set a 20KB limit for a few practical reasons:

  • Server load — small files are faster to upload and store in bulk
  • Slow connections — many applicants use mobile data or 2G/3G networks
  • Storage cost — portals store millions of applications, so small files add up
  • Legacy systems — older government systems often have hard file size limits

The most common portals requiring images under 20KB include UPSC, SSC CGL/CHSL, IBPS, Railway RRB, and various state PSC applications.

What If the Image Still Won't Go Below 20KB?

This happens when the image has very high resolution or complex detail. Here is what to do:

  • Resize first — use the Image Resizer to reduce dimensions to 275×354px or 413×531px (standard form sizes), then compress
  • Crop tightly — remove unnecessary background before compressing
  • Use JPG format — if your photo is PNG, convert it to JPG first, then compress. JPG compresses much smaller than PNG for photos

Common File Size Requirements for Indian Exams

Different exams have different requirements. Here are the most common ones:

  • UPSC — Photo: 20–300KB, Signature: 10–100KB
  • SSC CGL/CHSL — Photo: 20–50KB, Signature: 10–20KB
  • IBPS — Photo: 20–50KB, Signature: 10–20KB
  • Railway RRB — Photo: 15–40KB
  • NEET — Photo: 10–200KB (3.5cm × 4.5cm)
  • JEE Main — Photo: 10–200KB
  • JPSC — Photo: 20–50KB (275×354px)

Always check the official notification for the exact requirement before uploading.
