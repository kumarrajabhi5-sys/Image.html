About JPSC — Jharkhand Public Service Commission

The Jharkhand Public Service Commission (JPSC) is the state-level recruitment authority for Jharkhand government jobs. It conducts examinations for positions including JPSC Civil Services, Combined Graduate Level, and various departmental posts for candidates across Jharkhand — including Ranchi, Jamshedpur, Dhanbad, Bokaro, Hazaribagh, and all districts.

Every JPSC online application requires candidates to upload a recent passport-size photograph and a scanned signature in specific formats. Wrong photo size is one of the top reasons applications get rejected at the verification stage — even after payment is made.

Why JPSC Photo Upload Fails — Common Reasons

  • File too large — Raw phone photo is 2–5MB. JPSC allows max 50KB
  • Wrong format — uploading PNG or HEIC instead of JPG
  • Wrong dimensions — not matching the 200×250 pixel requirement
  • Dark or blurry photo — taken in poor lighting
  • Coloured background — JPSC requires white or light background
  • Old photo — photo must be recent (taken within 6 months)

How to Take a Correct Photo for JPSC at Home

You do not need a studio for JPSC photo. Here is how to take an acceptable photo at home using your smartphone:

  • Stand near a window for natural light — avoid direct sunlight
  • Use a plain white wall or hang a white bedsheet as background
  • Face the camera straight — no tilting or angle
  • Wear formal clothes — no cap, no sunglasses
  • Keep a neutral expression — slight smile is acceptable
  • Ask someone to take the photo — selfies are usually not accepted
