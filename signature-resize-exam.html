Why Is Signature Rejection So Common on Exam Portals?

Signature rejection is the second most common cause of failed exam registrations in India, after photo size errors. The problem is that most students don't know the exact pixel dimensions required. They take a photo of their signature on their phone and try to upload it directly — but the file is too large and the dimensions are wrong.

Each exam has different requirements. SSC wants 413×177 pixels under 20KB. IBPS wants 800×300 pixels under 20KB. UPSC wants under 60KB with specific cm dimensions. Getting this wrong means your application is rejected — sometimes without warning.

How to Scan Your Signature Properly for Exam Forms

You do not need a physical scanner. Here is the correct process using your phone:

  • Sign your name in black or blue ink on plain white A4 paper
  • Make sure the signature is bold and clearly visible — not faint
  • Take a photo in good lighting, flat on a table — no shadows
  • Crop the photo tightly around the signature before uploading to ExamReady
  • Do not use pencil — ink only

Common Signature Mistakes That Get Applications Rejected

  • Wrong file format — uploading PNG when JPG is required
  • File too large — uploading raw phone photo without compressing
  • Wrong dimensions — not matching the required pixel size
  • Faint or light signature — not visible after compression
  • Signature on coloured or lined paper instead of plain white
