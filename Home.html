Why Do Exam Portals Reject Your Images?

Every year, millions of Indian students face the frustrating problem of image rejection on exam registration portals. Whether it is NEET, JEE Main, UPSC, SSC CGL, or state board exams, almost every online application form requires you to upload your photograph, signature, and supporting documents in a very specific format.

The most common reasons for image rejection are: the file size is too large (exceeds the allowed KB limit), the image dimensions do not match the required pixels or centimetres, the file format is wrong (e.g., PNG instead of JPG), or the background colour is incorrect. ExamReady solves all of these problems in seconds.

How to Resize Photo for NEET 2024 Application Form

NEET UG requires your passport-size photograph to be in JPG format, sized between 10 KB and 200 KB, with dimensions of 3.5 cm × 4.5 cm. The signature must also be in JPG format, sized between 4 KB and 30 KB. Using ExamReady, you can select the NEET preset and all these values are automatically filled. Simply upload your photo and click Process.

How to Compress Image for JEE Main Registration

JEE Main application requires a photograph in JPG format with a file size between 10 KB and 100 KB. The image dimensions should be 3.5 cm × 4.5 cm with a white or off-white background. The signature must be in JPG format between 4 KB and 30 KB. ExamReady's JEE preset handles all of this automatically.

Image Size Requirements for UPSC Civil Services Exam

UPSC CSE requires a recent photograph in JPEG format, not exceeding 300 KB, with dimensions of 2 inches × 2 inches. The signature must be in JPEG format and not exceed 60 KB. ExamReady supports inch-based dimension input, making it the perfect tool for UPSC aspirants.
