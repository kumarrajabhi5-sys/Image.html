What is WebP and Why Does It Matter?

WebP is a modern image format developed by Google in 2010. It uses advanced compression technology that produces significantly smaller files compared to PNG and JPG — without any visible reduction in quality.

Google actively recommends WebP as part of its Core Web Vitals and PageSpeed scoring system. Websites that serve images in next-gen formats like WebP get a direct boost in their PageSpeed score, which can improve SEO rankings.

Does Converting to WebP Reduce Quality?

Not visibly. WebP uses lossy compression similar to JPG, but with a more efficient algorithm. At the same visual quality level, WebP produces a smaller file. At the same file size, WebP looks noticeably better than JPG.

For transparent images (logos, icons with transparent backgrounds), WebP also supports transparency like PNG — making it a true replacement for both formats.

Do All Browsers Support WebP?

Yes. As of 2024, WebP is supported by all major browsers — Chrome, Firefox, Safari (since iOS 14), Edge, and Opera. Over 97% of global users can view WebP images. It is completely safe to use WebP for your website images.

When Should You Still Use JPG or PNG?

  • Keep PNG for icons, logos, and screenshots where you need pixel-perfect sharpness
  • Keep JPG if your system or platform does not support WebP uploads (e.g. some older CMSs)
  • Use WebP for everything else — hero images, product photos, blog images, thumbnails
