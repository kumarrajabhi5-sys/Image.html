What Quality Setting Should You Use?

For government forms, the photo quality visible on screen matters less than you think — these photos are used for identification, not printing. A quality of 60–70% in JPG format will easily get your photo under 100KB while keeping your face clearly recognizable.

  • For photo under 100KB: use 65–75% quality
  • For photo under 50KB: use 50–60% quality
  • For signature under 20KB: use 40–50% quality (signatures are simple images, quality loss is minimal)

Should You Resize or Compress?

Most government forms specify both a file size limit AND pixel dimensions (like 200x230 pixels for passport photos). You may need to do both:

  1. First resize your image to the required pixel dimensions
  2. Then compress it to meet the file size limit

Resizing alone often reduces file size significantly. A photo resized from 3000x4000px to 200x230px will already be very small.
